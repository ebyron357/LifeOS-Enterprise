# Deliver Content-to-Lead Campaign

## Purpose

Move one approved commercial topic from research through published content, lead capture, nurture, measurement, and refresh.

## Trigger

A client or approved internal project selects one commercial service or product to grow.

## Inputs

- Approved client intake
- Required access invitations
- Baseline data
- Brand and compliance materials
- Commercial priority
- Ideal customer profile
- Reviewer and approval deadline
- Approved CTA
- Project owner

## Procedure

### 1. Confirm Scope

1. Identify the operating context and owner.
2. Select one commercial priority.
3. Define the primary audience, conversion goal, and CTA.
4. Confirm deliverables, deadlines, revision limit, and approval owner.
5. Record prohibited topics, regulated claims, and evidence requirements.

### 2. Establish Baseline

1. Record current search, traffic, conversion, email, and lead metrics.
2. Record top pages and current content.
3. Confirm analytics and Search Console access.
4. Identify existing lead magnets, forms, nurture, and CRM handoffs.
5. Save the baseline date and evidence locations.

### 3. Build Topic Cluster

1. Define the service or pillar page.
2. Select three to six supporting questions based on intent and commercial relevance.
3. Check for cannibalization.
4. Map internal links.
5. Select the lead-magnet format.
6. Map each asset to funnel stage and CTA.

### 4. Research and Brief

1. Research search intent, competitors, related questions, gaps, and evidence.
2. Complete the reader-intent sentence.
3. Create a Content Campaign record from the template.
4. Add original client expertise questions.
5. Capture approved answers and terminology.
6. Complete the brief and obtain approval.

### 5. Draft and Review

1. Produce the first draft using approved materials.
2. Flag every claim requiring verification.
3. Run accuracy, originality, usefulness, SEO, AEO/GEO, accessibility, and conversion QA.
4. Remove unsupported or generic material.
5. Send one consolidated client review request.
6. Apply approved revisions.
7. Record final approval.

### 6. Build Lead Magnet

1. Confirm format and outcome.
2. Create practical content that adds a checklist, worksheet, scorecard, template, or decision aid.
3. Apply approved brand design.
4. Export selectable-text PDF.
5. Run Acrobat quality and accessibility checks.
6. Record final file and version.

### 7. Build Funnel

1. Create the landing page.
2. Add sufficient public HTML value.
3. Configure the form and privacy language.
4. Configure thank-you page.
5. Configure delivery email.
6. Configure nurture emails.
7. Map fields, tags, source, and owner into CRM.
8. Define conversion events.

### 8. Publish and Test

1. Publish approved content.
2. Verify title, metadata, canonical, indexing, schema, images, links, and CTA.
3. Test desktop and mobile.
4. Submit or inspect in Search Console.
5. Test form submission.
6. Confirm CRM record.
7. Confirm delivery email and download.
8. Confirm nurture starts correctly.
9. Confirm analytics events fire.
10. Add internal links from relevant pages.

### 9. Launch Lead Support

1. Use the Lead Acquisition SOP for approved prospecting.
2. Connect outreach only to a verified gap and relevant proof asset.
3. Record source and campaign.
4. Hand qualified replies to the CRM owner.

### 10. Monitor and Report

1. Create 30-day monitoring task.
2. Create 90-day refresh task.
3. Record production, search, funnel, lead, and revenue metrics.
4. Report work completed, changes, leads, issues, and next actions.
5. Update content based on evidence.

## Expected Result

One complete, approved, measurable content-to-lead campaign with published content, lead magnet, working funnel, CRM handoff, attribution fields, and scheduled review.

## Validation Checklist

- [ ] Scope, audience, goal, and CTA approved
- [ ] Baseline recorded
- [ ] Topic cluster documented
- [ ] Client expertise included
- [ ] Claims verified
- [ ] Content approved
- [ ] Lead magnet passed QA
- [ ] Landing page and form tested
- [ ] Delivery and nurture tested
- [ ] CRM handoff verified
- [ ] Analytics verified
- [ ] Review dates scheduled
- [ ] No unsupported claims or private data exposed

## Exceptions and Troubleshooting

- Missing analytics: document baseline limitation and configure tracking before claiming impact.
- Delayed approval: move to `client-input-required` and escalate to the approval owner.
- Regulated claim: remove or obtain qualified review.
- Form failure: stop promotion until end-to-end delivery works.
- Duplicate page: consolidate or revise cluster before publishing.
- Weak lead magnet: choose a more practical format rather than expanding length.
- Unclear attribution: label as unknown or partial; do not overclaim.
