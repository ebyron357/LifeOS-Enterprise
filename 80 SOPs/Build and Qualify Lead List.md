# Build and Qualify Lead List

## Purpose

Build a small, verified, relevant list of business accounts and decision-makers for a reviewed Content-to-Lead outreach campaign.

## Trigger

A pilot niche, offer, ideal client profile, and outreach proof asset have been approved.

## Inputs

- Ideal client profile
- Geography and exclusions
- Approved offer
- Relevant case study, audit, checklist, or proof asset
- CRM or approved tracker
- Suppression list
- Outreach owner
- Campaign target size
- Jurisdiction and policy review where needed

## Procedure

### 1. Define the Campaign

1. Select one niche.
2. Select one location or account segment.
3. Define company-size or maturity criteria.
4. Define observable content, website, lead-capture, or follow-up gaps.
5. Select one or two decision-maker roles.
6. Define exclusion and suppression rules.
7. Set the pilot list size, normally 25–50 accounts.
8. Record the offer and CTA.

### 2. Discover Accounts

Use one or more approved sources:

- Google Maps or official Google Places API for local businesses;
- Apollo account search for B2B markets;
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator for professional context;
- approved directories or associations;
- existing referrals and relationships.

For every account:

1. Record exact source and URL.
2. Confirm official business website.
3. Confirm service, location, and basic fit.
4. Check suppression and existing relationship status.
5. Reject accounts that do not meet the ICP.

### 3. Research the Gap

1. Review the official website.
2. Identify one current, specific gap or opportunity.
3. Save the evidence URL.
4. Avoid subjective or insulting language.
5. State what is observed and what remains unknown.
6. Connect the gap to one relevant service or proof asset.

Examples:

- no clear page for a profitable service;
- useful content without CTAs;
- no lead magnet;
- form without visible delivery or next step;
- weak topic cluster;
- outdated content;
- no conversion measurement evident;
- disconnected content and nurture.

### 4. Score the Account

Use the 100-point score:

- ICP fit: 20
- Commercial opportunity: 15
- Observable gap: 20
- Decision-maker access: 10
- Data confidence: 10
- Urgency or trigger: 10
- Relevant proof: 10
- Compliance and reputation fit: 5

Route:

- 80–100: priority review
- 65–79: more research or nurture
- 50–64: low priority
- below 50: reject/archive

### 5. Discover the Contact

1. Use Apollo, LinkedIn, company team pages, or approved Clay enrichment sources.
2. Confirm current company and role.
3. Select the person most likely to own the problem.
4. Do not contact several people at one account without an account strategy.
5. Record contact source and date.
6. Leave fields blank when unknown.

### 6. Enrich and Verify

1. Use Apollo or Clay only after account qualification.
2. Run the minimum enrichment needed.
3. Record provider, date, confidence, and raw/normalized value.
4. Verify work email with an approved source.
5. Mark risky, invalid, accept-all, or unavailable results accurately.
6. Never mark consent based on enrichment.
7. Do not add personal data unrelated to the business purpose.

### 7. Create the Prospect Record

1. Create a note from `99 Templates/Lead Prospect.md`.
2. Fill account, source, gap, evidence, score, contact, verification, suppression, owner, and next action.
3. Link the campaign and project.
4. Record CRM ID when handed off.
5. Keep private exports outside tracked Git files.

### 8. Prepare Personalization

1. Use the verified observation.
2. Explain the commercial relevance.
3. Select one helpful proof asset or suggestion.
4. Do not fabricate familiarity, relationships, urgency, or results.
5. Keep one clear offer and CTA.
6. Include identity and opt-out requirements.

### 9. Human Approval

Before sending:

- [ ] Account and contact verified
- [ ] Suppression clear
- [ ] Observation current and evidenced
- [ ] Offer relevant
- [ ] Proof accurate
- [ ] Claims reviewed
- [ ] Jurisdiction and platform rules reviewed where required
- [ ] Opt-out included where required
- [ ] Sender and reply owner confirmed
- [ ] CRM or tracking record exists

### 10. Send Small Pilot

1. Send a small reviewed batch.
2. Monitor bounces immediately.
3. Stop if deliverability or complaint signals are unacceptable.
4. Honor opt-outs immediately.
5. Classify replies.
6. Hand positive or referral responses to the owner.
7. Record next action and due date.

### 11. Review Results

Track:

- accounts discovered and qualified;
- contact match rate;
- verified email rate;
- bounce rate;
- reply and positive-reply rate;
- opt-out rate;
- meetings;
- proposals;
- customers;
- revenue;
- data corrections;
- time and cost per qualified opportunity.

Update the ICP, scoring, offer, and message only from evidence.

## Expected Result

A small, verified, source-documented lead list with relevant outreach reasons, appropriate contacts, suppression controls, CRM handoff, and measurable outcomes.

## Validation Checklist

- [ ] ICP and exclusions approved
- [ ] Source and URLs recorded
- [ ] Official website verified
- [ ] Gap supported by evidence
- [ ] Score completed
- [ ] Contact role confirmed
- [ ] Email verification state recorded
- [ ] Suppression checked
- [ ] Message reviewed
- [ ] Opt-outs honored
- [ ] CRM handoff complete
- [ ] Outcome and attribution recorded

## Exceptions and Troubleshooting

- Google result without official website: mark review required; do not infer domain.
- Apollo contact appears outdated: verify on LinkedIn or company website before use.
- Clay sources conflict: preserve both values and escalate for human review.
- Accept-all domain: mark risky; use another channel or additional verification.
- No appropriate decision-maker: keep at account level or reject.
- Unclear legal basis: do not send until reviewed.
- High bounce/opt-out rate: pause campaign and audit data, offer, copy, and sending setup.
- Client-owned list: keep isolated and follow client instructions and permissions.
