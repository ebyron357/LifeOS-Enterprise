# Physical Health and Mobility

## Purpose

Protect mobility, reduce avoidable flare-ups, maintain strength, and prepare for safe rehabilitation under medical supervision.

## Known Constraints

- Five herniated discs in the lower back
- One herniated disc in the neck
- MRI-confirmed spinal damage
- Chronic sciatica
- Three prior knee surgeries
- Ongoing back and knee pain
- Back surgery planned or pending recovery clearance

## Non-Negotiable Safety Rules

- The surgeon, physician, or physical therapist determines what movements are permitted.
- No exercise should create sharp pain, increased radiating pain, new numbness, tingling, weakness, joint instability, or loss of function.
- Do not push through pain to complete a set.
- Avoid loaded spinal flexion, twisting under load, high-impact exercise, deep knee bending, jumping, running, and heavy lifting unless specifically cleared.
- Use stable support for standing movements.
- Stop the session when form changes because of pain or fatigue.

## Emergency Red Flags

Seek emergency medical evaluation for new or worsening loss of bladder or bowel control, inability to urinate, numbness in the saddle/genital area, or significant/progressive arm or leg weakness.

## Current Health Outcomes

1. Maintain safe daily movement without increasing neurological symptoms.
2. Build gentle upper-body and core endurance while protecting the spine.
3. Strengthen the muscles supporting the knees without high joint load.
4. Improve consistency, not intensity.
5. Enter formal rehabilitation with a clear symptom and activity record.

## Tracking

Record after every session:

- Starting pain: 0–10
- Ending pain: 0–10
- Sciatica or radiating symptoms: none / same / increased
- Knee swelling or instability: none / same / increased
- Exercises completed
- Symptoms later that day and the next morning

## Clinical Review Questions

- Which spinal movements are restricted before surgery?
- Am I cleared for resistance bands or light weights?
- Is a recumbent bicycle, pool exercise, or walking appropriate?
- What pain or neurological change requires stopping immediately?
- Which exercises should be used for prehabilitation?
- What will the post-operative rehabilitation timeline permit?
