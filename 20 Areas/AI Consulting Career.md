# AI Consulting Career & Portfolio

## Purpose

Build a credible, revenue-ready portfolio for consulting with organizations on AI strategy, workflow automation, agent implementation, adoption, and governance.

## Operating Rule

A certification is not complete until it produces **proof of capability**. Every credential must be paired with at least one portfolio artifact, case study, demonstration, workshop, assessment, or client-ready deliverable.

## Consulting Positioning

Primary positioning:

> AI transformation and automation consultant who helps organizations identify high-value AI opportunities, redesign workflows, deploy practical agents and automations, and establish responsible operating controls.

This positioning may be refined by market evidence. Do not lock into an industry or claim client results without proof.

## Credential Strategy

Use a layered credential strategy rather than collecting unrelated certificates.

1. **Business transformation** — prove the ability to identify AI opportunities and lead adoption.
2. **Cross-cloud AI literacy** — communicate credibly across Microsoft, Google Cloud, and AWS environments.
3. **Agent implementation** — prove the ability to design, integrate, test, and manage AI agents.
4. **Governance and risk** — demonstrate responsible AI, policy, controls, and lifecycle oversight.
5. **Consulting delivery** — discovery, process mapping, ROI modeling, change management, training, and executive communication.

## Current Priority Order

| Priority | Credential | Purpose | Decision |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Microsoft Certified: AI Transformation Leader (AB-731) | Directly supports AI strategy, opportunity identification, adoption planning, business value, and responsible implementation | Take first |
| 2 | Google Cloud Generative AI Leader | Adds vendor-neutral-looking business credibility and strengthens business strategy discussions | Take second |
| 3 | AWS Certified AI Practitioner | Adds practical AI, security, compliance, governance, and AWS ecosystem literacy | Take third |
| 4 | Microsoft Certified: AI Agent Builder Associate (AB-620) | Provides stronger implementation proof for enterprise agents, APIs, connectors, RAG, MCP, and multi-agent solutions | Take after building two working agents |
| 5 | IAPP Artificial Intelligence Governance Professional (AIGP) | Adds recognized governance, law, lifecycle, risk, and responsible-AI credibility | Take after the initial consulting portfolio is producing revenue or a target opportunity specifically values governance |

## Certifications Not Prioritized Yet

- **Microsoft AI Business Professional (AB-730):** useful, but substantially overlaps the first-stage business AI track. Take only if free, discounted, required by a target opportunity, or needed as a quick confidence-building credential.
- Generic prompt-engineering certificates without a proctored assessment or practical portfolio evidence.
- Expensive private certifications that cannot show employer recognition, an independent credential registry, or verifiable assessment standards.

## Skill Pillars

### 1. AI Strategy

- AI readiness assessment
- Opportunity discovery
- Use-case prioritization
- ROI and cost modeling
- Build-versus-buy analysis
- Roadmap creation

### 2. Workflow and Automation

- Process discovery
- Current-state and future-state mapping
- Automation design
- Human approval points
- Failure handling
- Measurement and optimization

### 3. Agent Implementation

- Prompt and instruction architecture
- Knowledge sources and retrieval
- APIs, connectors, MCP, and tool use
- Testing and evaluation
- Access control and permissions
- Monitoring and fallback workflows

### 4. Governance and Risk

- Responsible AI principles
- AI inventory
- Risk classification
- Vendor assessment
- Data, privacy, security, and intellectual-property controls
- Policies, review cadence, incident response, and evidence

### 5. Consulting Delivery

- Discovery interviews
- Executive presentations
- Facilitation and workshops
- Proposals and statements of work
- Change management
- Training and documentation
- Case-study development

## Required Portfolio Proof

Maintain at least the following public or sanitized proof:

1. AI Readiness Assessment template and completed example.
2. AI Opportunity Map with impact, feasibility, cost, risk, and priority scoring.
3. Workflow Transformation case study showing the before state, intervention, and measurable result.
4. Working AI agent or automation demonstration with architecture, safety controls, and evaluation results.
5. Responsible AI starter policy, AI inventory, and risk register.
6. Executive AI Adoption Roadmap and presentation.
7. Three case studies with clear context labels: personal, internal venture, client-approved, nonprofit, research, or demonstration.
8. LinkedIn and portfolio pages with verifiable credentials, demonstrations, and outcomes.

## Weekly Review

- Which credential is active?
- What practical artifact will it produce?
- How many focused study hours were completed?
- What market evidence changed this week?
- Which portfolio item moved closer to publishable quality?
- Which business problem can now be solved more credibly?
- Which certificate or course should be delayed because it lacks immediate application?

## Evidence Standard

Every recommended certification must record:

- official credential source;
- employer or market signal;
- confidence level;
- cost and renewal requirements;
- target consulting service;
- practical artifact;
- review date;
- evidence status: Verified, Partially Verified, Unverified, Blocked by Missing Access, Requires Account Inspection, or Requires Live Testing.
