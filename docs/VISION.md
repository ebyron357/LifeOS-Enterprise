## The Problem

Modern knowledge workers and ambitious individuals face a compound productivity problem:

- **Fragmented tools** — Notes in one app, tasks in another, goals elsewhere, and no connective tissue between them.
- **Capture without retrieval** — Information is collected but never surfaced when it matters.
- **No review cadence** — Plans are made, never revisited, and drift into irrelevance.
- **Context switching cost** — Moving between tools breaks flow and erodes deep work time.
- **Scaling chaos** — Systems that work at low volume collapse under the weight of real life.

The result: high-effort, low-output systems that people abandon within months.

---

## The Vision

LifeOS Enterprise is a **personal operating system** — a single, structured environment where every domain of life (work, health, finance, relationships, learning, goals) is managed with the discipline and reliability of enterprise software.

The system provides:

- **One place** for every note, project, goal, habit, person, and decision
- **Consistent structure** that makes information easy to find, query, and review
- **Automation** that handles routine operations without manual effort
- **AI augmentation** that surfaces relevant information and assists synthesis
- **Review workflows** that keep the system alive and aligned with current priorities

The end state is a system that reduces cognitive overhead, increases intentionality, and compounds value over time.

---

## Success Criteria

The system is successful when:

1. **Capture is frictionless** — Any piece of information can be captured in under 10 seconds.
2. **Retrieval is reliable** — Any note can be found within 30 seconds without remembering where it was filed.
3. **Reviews happen consistently** — Daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly reviews are completed reliably.
4. **Projects don't fall through the cracks** — Every active project has a clear next action and a review date.
5. **The system grows with use** — More notes and more time make the system more valuable, not more cluttered.
6. **Maintenance is minimal** — The system requires less than 30 minutes of maintenance per week beyond normal use.
7. **It survives real life** — The system remains functional during high-stress periods when manual curation drops to near zero.

---

## Non-Goals

LifeOS Enterprise explicitly does **not** aim to:

- Replace dedicated tools for deep domain work (e.g., accounting software, project management platforms for teams)
- Serve as a publishing or blogging platform
- Be a social or collaborative tool
- Automate decision-making — it supports decisions, not replaces them
- Be the "perfect" system — it aims to be the *right* system for one person's real life

---

## Guiding Metaphors

**The Operating System:** Like a computer OS, LifeOS runs beneath everything else. It doesn't do the work — it provides the environment in which work happens reliably.

**The Second Brain:** The system externalizes memory and retrieval, freeing cognitive capacity for higher-order thinking.

**The Enterprise System:** The same discipline applied to Fortune 500 data architecture — schemas, types, relationships, audits — applied to one person's life.
