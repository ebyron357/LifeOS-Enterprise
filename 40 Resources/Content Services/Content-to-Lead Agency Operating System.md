# Content-to-Lead Agency Operating System

## Purpose

Operate a complete content and lead-generation service for website, digital, consulting, and approved client engagements.

Do not sell disconnected blog posts or ebooks. Sell a measurable system:

```text
Search question
  → useful page or article
  → lead magnet
  → email capture
  → delivery and nurture
  → consultation, quote, or sale
  → analytics and refresh
```

## 1. Business Model

Each major client topic should eventually include:

1. One primary service or pillar page
2. Three to six supporting articles
3. One downloadable guide, checklist, template, workbook, scorecard, or calculator
4. One lead-magnet landing page
5. One email capture form
6. One delivery email
7. Two or three nurture emails
8. Search, conversion, and attribution tracking
9. A performance-review date

The service is successful when content creates a measurable path to a qualified business outcome.

## 4. Client Onboarding

### Intake

Collect:

- company name and website;
- services and products;
- service locations;
- primary conversion goal;
- highest-value services;
- services the client wants to grow;
- customer value and buying process;
- competitors;
- ideal customer and objections;
- qualifications and evidence;
- proprietary process;
- case studies and testimonials;
- approved claims;
- regulated or prohibited topics;
- brand voice and terminology;
- CTA preferences;
- approval owner and final decision-maker;
- review deadline and revision limit.

### Access

Request invitations rather than passwords where possible:

- CMS
- GA4
- Search Console
- email platform
- CRM
- form builder
- booking platform
- Canva brand kit
- existing SEO tool
- image library
- analytics and reporting tools

Do not request passwords through ordinary email.

### Baseline

Record before work begins:

- organic traffic;
- impressions and clicks;
- indexed pages;
- top pages;
- current rankings;
- qualified leads;
- landing-page conversion rate;
- email-list size;
- lead-magnet performance;
- email open and click rates;
- conversion events;
- existing lead sources.

## 5. Controlled Client AI Workspace

Create one governed AI workspace per client.

Add only approved materials:

- brand guidelines;
- service descriptions;
- approved copy;
- customer profiles;
- high-quality prior content;
- case studies and testimonials;
- approved sources;
- SEO strategy;
- legal and compliance restrictions;
- competitor notes;
- CTA rules;
- internal-link map.

### Standard Instructions

- Use approved client terminology.
- Do not invent facts, prices, qualifications, stories, testimonials, statistics, or results.
- Flag information requiring verification.
- Prioritize original client expertise.
- Answer directly before lengthy explanation.
- Use descriptive headings based on real customer questions.
- Include limitations and tradeoffs.
- Suggest internal links only when the destination is known.
- Distinguish confirmed facts from recommendations.
- Avoid filler and keyword stuffing.
- Write for the reader first.

## 7. Content Strategy

### Select One Commercial Priority

Begin with the service the client most wants to sell.

### Build a Topic Cluster

Each cluster contains:

- one service or pillar page;
- supporting educational, comparison, cost, process, and decision content;
- one lead magnet;
- internal links connecting the cluster;
- one primary conversion action.

### Funnel Mapping

| Stage | Content | Primary action |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Educational article | Read related guide |
| Consideration | Comparison or how-to | Download checklist |
| Decision | Cost, service, case study | Request quote or call |
| Conversion | Service or landing page | Submit form |
| Nurture | Email sequence | Reply, book, or buy |

Topics must support a customer decision, service, or conversion—not search volume alone.

## 8. Blog Production SOP

### Research Gate

Record:

- primary query;
- search intent;
- difficulty and volume when available;
- related questions;
- competitors;
- missing topics;
- location modifiers;
- internal-link opportunities;
- commercial relevance;
- conversion opportunity.

Do not draft until this sentence is complete:

> The reader is searching for this because they need to ______ before they can ______.

### Content Brief

Include:

- audience;
- primary question;
- search intent;
- reader situation and outcome;
- keyword and related terms;
- outline;
- original client insight;
- evidence and sources;
- internal links;
- CTA;
- related lead magnet;
- approval and compliance needs;
- differentiation.

### Expertise Capture

Ask five to ten focused questions about:

- mistakes customers make;
- what competitors omit;
- price and timeline factors;
- when the service is not appropriate;
- process;
- examples;
- realistic outcomes;
- warning signs;
- misunderstandings;
- recommendations and tradeoffs.

### First Draft Requirements

- Answer the primary question early.
- Match search intent.
- Use one H1 and descriptive H2s.
- Include client insight and examples.
- Explain steps logically.
- Include limitations and tradeoffs.
- Use only known internal links.
- Include the approved CTA.
- Flag statements requiring fact-checking.
- Do not invent evidence.
- Avoid filler, repetition, and keyword stuffing.

### Internal QA

Review:

- accuracy;
- originality;
- client-specific expertise;
- usefulness;
- SEO;
- AEO/GEO structure;
- accessibility;
- conversion path;
- factual and compliance risk.

### Client Review

One primary reviewer, one approval deadline, consolidated feedback, and a defined revision limit.

### Publishing Gate

Confirm:

- H1 and heading hierarchy;
- title, description, and slug;
- image naming and alt text;
- internal and external links;
- author and update date;
- CTA and lead magnet;
- responsive layout;
- form and delivery;
- structured data;
- analytics events;
- indexing and canonical;
- Open Graph;
- no broken links or placeholders.

After publishing:

- test desktop and mobile;
- inspect in Search Console;
- test forms and delivery;
- confirm tracking;
- add internal links;
- schedule review.

## 9. Lead-Magnet Production

Select the format based on the user need:

| Need | Format |
|---|---|
| Complete a process | Checklist |
| Make a decision | Comparison guide |
| Plan a project | Workbook |
| Avoid mistakes | Warning-sign guide |
| Calculate | Worksheet or calculator |
| Prepare for consultation | Planning guide |
| Repeat a method | Template |
| Evaluate a provider | Scorecard |
| Understand a complex topic | Ebook |

Required structure:

1. Cover
2. Outcome-focused introduction
3. Audience
4. Framework or direct answer
5. Steps
6. Checklist, worksheet, template, or scorecard
7. Mistakes
8. Next steps
9. CTA
10. About the author or company
11. Sources where appropriate

Do not paste blog posts into a PDF. Add a practical process, exercise, decision aid, or usable template.

### PDF QA

Check:

- selectable text;
- document title, author, and language;
- headings and reading order;
- hyperlinks;
- alt text;
- accessibility tags;
- file size and mobile readability;
- page order;
- fonts and images.

## 10. Landing Page, Delivery, and Nurture

Landing page:

- outcome-focused H1;
- useful introduction;
- three to five benefits;
- contents;
- audience;
- concise form;
- privacy statement;
- trust and credibility;
- FAQ;
- final CTA.

Delivery workflow:

```text
Form submitted
  → contact added or updated
  → source and interest recorded
  → delivery email sent
  → thank-you page displayed
  → nurture sequence begins
  → conversion recorded
```

Nurture sequence:

1. Immediate delivery and instructions
2. Practical lesson and common mistake
3. Service connection and next action
4. Optional approved case study
5. Optional objection handling

## 11. Lead Acquisition and Enrichment

Use the linked Lead Acquisition and Enrichment Playbook.

The lead workflow is:

```text
Ideal client profile
  → account discovery
  → company qualification
  → contact discovery
  → enrichment and verification
  → personalization research
  → reviewed outreach
  → reply classification
  → CRM handoff
  → follow-up
  → attribution
```

Do not treat scraped contact data as consent. Maintain suppression, opt-out, duplicate, and jurisdiction review rules.

## 12. SEO, AEO, and GEO Standards

### SEO

- one primary purpose;
- intent alignment;
- descriptive metadata;
- heading structure;
- internal links;
- credible sources;
- mobile performance;
- accessibility;
- correct indexing;
- conversion path.

### AEO

- direct answers;
- question-based headings;
- definitions;
- numbered steps;
- structured comparisons;
- concise, self-contained sections.

### GEO

- original expertise;
- clear authorship;
- credible sources;
- consistent entities and terminology;
- examples;
- limitations;
- update dates;
- public HTML;
- accurate claims.

SEO, AEO, and GEO are operating disciplines, not a single tool or hidden setting.

## 13. Automation

Automate stable handoffs:

- intake submission to project creation;
- status change to review notification;
- form submission to CRM and delivery;
- publication to monitoring task;
- review date to refresh task;
- qualified response to owner notification.

Do not automate:

- factual approval;
- subject-matter expertise;
- final editorial judgment;
- compliance review;
- sensitive claims;
- regulated advice;
- public release of unverified results.

## 15. Weekly Operating Schedule

### Monday

Analytics, priorities, keyword research, competitors, and briefs.

### Tuesday

Expert interviews, drafts, lead-magnet copy, and prospect-list research.

### Wednesday

Editorial QA, SEO/AEO/GEO review, graphics, enrichment, and personalization research.

Approvals, revisions, CMS formatting, forms, emails, outreach review, and funnel testing.

### Friday

Publishing, reporting, reviewed outreach, follow-up, monitoring, and next-week preparation.

## 16. Tool Governance

Start with one primary tool per job. Add another only when a repeated limitation, workload, client requirement, or ROI case is documented.

Do not subscribe simultaneously to multiple overlapping writing and SEO platforms without evidence.

## 17. Quality Rules

Never publish content that:

- contains invented facts or proof;
- copies competitors;
- uses unsupported statistics;
- hides all value behind a form;
- exists only for word count;
- lacks audience, purpose, CTA, or review;
- uses fake experience, quotes, stories, or results;
- gives regulated advice without qualified review;
- creates cannibalization;
- is outdated without disclosure.

The goal is not to disguise AI use. The goal is accurate, useful, differentiated work based on real expertise and reviewed evidence.

## Definition of Done

The system is operational when one topic can move from research to a measurable conversion without rebuilding the process, losing approvals, inventing evidence, or switching between unnecessary tools.
