# AI Portfolio Showcase Plan

## Purpose

Build a credible, evidence-based AI consulting portfolio that shows what Bwa can assess, design, build, govern, explain, and improve. The portfolio must help a business decision-maker understand the problem solved, the method used, the proof available, and the next step to engage.

The portfolio is not a gallery of screenshots and it is not a list of AI tools. It is a structured proof system.

## Primary Outcome

By October 31, 2026, publish a professional AI consulting portfolio with:

- one clear positioning statement;
- three flagship case studies;
- at least two working demonstrations;
- one AI readiness assessment sample;
- one AI adoption roadmap sample;
- one governance and risk sample;
- verified credentials and badges;
- a services page with a clear next action;
- a repeatable publishing system across GitHub, LinkedIn, Substack, and video;
- a private proof register that separates public evidence from confidential work.

## Portfolio Positioning

### Working Position

> AI operations and transformation consultant who helps organizations identify high-value AI opportunities, design practical workflows, implement reliable systems, and establish the governance needed to use AI responsibly.

This statement must be refined after the first three case studies are complete. Do not narrow to a specific industry until the evidence shows a defensible pattern.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- small and midsize business owners;
- operators and department leaders;
- agencies and consultants needing AI implementation support;
- ecommerce and service-business teams;
- organizations evaluating AI readiness, automation, agents, and governance.

Secondary audience:

- hiring managers;
- strategic partners;
- event organizers;
- nonprofit and education partners;
- professional communities.

## Proof Hierarchy

Use proof in this order:

1. **Verified production result** — measured outcome with permission to publish.
2. **Verified internal result** — measured internal outcome accurately labeled.
3. **Working demonstration** — functioning system with test evidence, labeled as a demonstration.
4. **Architecture and decision artifact** — assessment, roadmap, process map, risk register, or evaluation.
5. **Credential** — verified certification or badge linked to an applied artifact.
6. **Thought leadership** — explanation, tutorial, or analysis supported by real work.
7. **Claim without evidence** — prohibited.

## Portfolio Architecture

### 1. Home / Executive Overview

Must answer in under 30 seconds:

- What does Bwa help organizations do?
- What proof is available?
- What services can be purchased?
- What is the next action?

Required sections:

- positioning statement;
- three featured case studies;
- core capabilities;
- verified credentials;
- selected proof metrics;
- services;
- contact or discovery call action.

### 2. Case Studies

Every public case study must use the case-study standard in this document and include an explicit context label.

Allowed context labels:

- Personal
- Internal Venture
- Client-Approved
- Nonprofit
- Research
- Demonstration
- Open Source

### 3. Demonstrations

Each demonstration must include:

- short video or annotated walkthrough;
- architecture diagram;
- tools and data sources;
- human approval points;
- testing method;
- known limitations;
- repository or technical evidence where safe;
- next improvement.

### 4. Services

Initial productized service categories:

1. AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment
2. AI Workflow and Automation Design
3. AI Agent or Knowledge System Implementation
4. AI Governance, Risk, and Evaluation Starter Pack
5. Ongoing AI Operations and Optimization

Pricing is not considered final until it is validated through discovery conversations and documented in LifeOS.

### 5. Credentials

Each credential must link to:

- verification URL;
- skills measured;
- practical artifact produced;
- case study or demonstration that uses the skill;
- renewal date when applicable.

### 6. Insights

Use Substack and LinkedIn to explain practical decisions from the work:

- what problem was found;
- how options were evaluated;
- why one approach was selected;
- what failed;
- what changed after testing;
- what a business leader should do next.

### 7. Contact and Conversion

Every public proof asset must lead to one next action:

- request an AI readiness assessment;
- book a discovery conversation;
- request a capability statement;
- view a related case study;
- subscribe to Substack;
- connect on LinkedIn.

## Flagship Selection Rules

Select the first three case studies using this score:

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Evidence strength | 25 |
| Relevance to target buyer | 20 |
| Demonstrates a paid consulting capability | 20 |
| Working or production readiness | 15 |
| Permission and privacy readiness | 10 |
| Visual and storytelling strength | 5 |
| Ability to produce public content | 5 |

Do not choose a project solely because it looks impressive. Choose work that proves a service a business can buy.

## Case Study Standard

Every case study must contain:

### Executive Summary

- Context label
- One-sentence problem
- One-sentence solution
- Evidence status
- Measured or test result
- Role performed

### Business Problem

- Who experienced the problem?
- What process was affected?
- What was the baseline?
- What was the cost, delay, risk, or missed opportunity?

### Constraints

- Budget
- Timeline
- Data access
- Privacy
- Security
- Compliance
- Technical environment
- Human approval requirements

### Before Workflow

Show the original workflow as a clear sequence.

### Solution

- Architecture
- Models and platforms
- Knowledge sources
- Tools, APIs, MCP servers, and connectors
- Human approval points
- Permissions
- Retry and fallback behavior
- Monitoring and evaluation

### Implementation

- What was built?
- What changed?
- What was tested?
- What was rejected or revised?

### Results

Use only verified results. Acceptable forms include:

- completion time;
- first-pass success rate;
- human editing time;
- production success rate;
- error reduction;
- lead response time;
- task completion rate;
- cost per deliverable;
- reliability;
- user satisfaction;
- qualitative result clearly labeled as qualitative.

### Evidence

- Repository or commit
- Deployment
- Screenshot
- Test report
- Audit
- Workflow log
- Video
- Client approval
- Credential relationship

### Limits and Lessons

- What did not work?
- What remains unverified?
- What would be improved next?
- What should not be generalized from this example?

### Call to Action

One next action related to the demonstrated capability.

## Portfolio Evidence Gate

A case study may be marked `ready-to-publish` only when:

- [ ] Context is correctly labeled.
- [ ] Ownership and publishing rights are confirmed.
- [ ] Private information is removed.
- [ ] Results have an evidence source.
- [ ] Claims match the evidence.
- [ ] Architecture is understandable.
- [ ] Limitations are disclosed.
- [ ] Screenshots are current and readable.
- [ ] Links work.
- [ ] One conversion action is present.
- [ ] Review date is recorded.

## Channel Strategy

### Portfolio Website

Role: canonical professional proof and conversion destination.

Publish:

- executive positioning;
- case studies;
- demonstrations;
- services;
- credentials;
- capability statement;
- contact action.

### GitHub

Role: technical verification.

Publish where safe:

- clean public repositories;
- architecture documentation;
- README case-study summaries;
- test evidence;
- release notes;
- sample code;
- demonstration environments.

Do not expose secrets, client data, private repositories, or unlicensed work.

### LinkedIn

Role: authority, networking, discovery, and distribution.

For each case study create:

- one launch post;
- one problem/lesson post;
- one architecture or workflow visual;
- one short result or reflection post;
- one Featured-section link.

### Substack

Role: long-form analysis and searchable thought leadership.

For each flagship case study publish one article covering:

- the business problem;
- decision process;
- architecture;
- what failed;
- practical lessons;
- next-step checklist.

### Video

Role: make working proof understandable.

For each flagship case study create:

- 60–90 second overview;
- 3–7 minute walkthrough;
- optional longer technical explanation.

### Capability Statement

Role: one-page sales asset.

Include:

- positioning;
- core services;
- selected proof;
- credentials;
- operating approach;
- contact information.

### Skool and Professional Communities

Role: teaching, feedback, relationship building, and lead generation.

Share useful lessons and demonstrations without turning every interaction into a sales pitch.

## 12-Week Execution Plan

### Weeks 1–2 — Inventory and Positioning

- Inventory all candidate work.
- Assign context, ownership, permission, evidence, and publish status.
- Score candidates.
- Select the first three flagship case studies.
- Draft the positioning statement and capability categories.

### Weeks 3–4 — Build the Proof System

- Create the case-study template.
- Create the portfolio proof register.
- Create the native Obsidian Base.
- Gather screenshots, diagrams, commits, deployments, tests, and metrics.
- Identify missing proof and schedule tests.

### Weeks 5–7 — Produce Flagship Case Study 1

- Complete the written case study.
- Create the demonstration video.
- Clean or prepare the related GitHub proof.
- Publish the portfolio page.
- Repurpose to LinkedIn and Substack.

### Weeks 8–9 — Produce Flagship Case Study 2

Repeat the complete proof and publishing process.

### Weeks 10–11 — Produce Flagship Case Study 3

Repeat the complete proof and publishing process.

### Week 12 — Convert Proof Into Opportunities

- Publish capability statement.
- Update LinkedIn Featured section.
- Create a targeted outreach list.
- Send proof-led outreach.
- Conduct discovery calls.
- Track which case studies generate engagement and conversations.
- Revise positioning based on evidence.

## Portfolio Scorecard

Score each public asset from 0–5:

- Business relevance
- Evidence strength
- Clarity
- Technical credibility
- Governance and risk clarity
- Visual quality
- Buyer usefulness
- Conversion path
- Accessibility
- Freshness

Minimum publish score: **40/50**, with no zero in evidence, privacy, or claim accuracy.

## Review Rhythm

### Weekly

- Review the next proof action.
- Review blocked evidence.
- Review permission status.
- Review publishing queue.

### Monthly

- Review target roles and buyer demand.
- Review which proof receives engagement.
- Review which projects should be added, paused, or removed.
- Verify all public links and claims.

### Quarterly

- Replace outdated demonstrations.
- Refresh metrics and screenshots.
- Archive weak or irrelevant case studies.
- Update positioning and services based on actual opportunities.

## Definition of Done

The portfolio system is operational when Bwa can answer in under one minute:

- Which three case studies are the flagship proofs?
- What evidence is missing from each?
- Which items are safe and approved to publish?
- Where is each proof published?
- Which capabilities does each project demonstrate?
- Which credential supports each capability?
- What content should be produced next?
- Which proof is creating professional conversations or leads?

## Immediate Next Action

Classify and score the candidate case studies by context, ownership, permission, evidence strength, consulting relevance, and readiness. Do not begin public design work until the first three flagship proofs are selected.
